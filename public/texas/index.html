Property tax in Texas is governed by the Texas Property Tax Code, Chapter 41 (Local Review / Protest) and Chapter 42 (Judicial Review) (Tex. Tax Code Ch. 41 (§§ 41.41 Right of Protest, 41.44 Notice of Protest); Ch. 42 (judicial appeal); Ch. 23 (appraisal, incl. § 23.23 residence homestead cap)). Assessment cycle: Annual. Property is appraised each year as of January 1 by the county appraisal district (CAD); notices of appraised value are typically mailed in April/May. Assessed value: Texas requires appraisal at 100% of market value as of January 1 (full-value assessment; no fractional statutory ratio). A separate 10% annual cap on the increase in appraised value applies to residence homesteads under Tex. Tax Code § 23.23 (taxable value cannot rise more than 10% per year, plus new improvements).
